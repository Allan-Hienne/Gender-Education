A part of feminism at Disney

Poster of the movie "Beauty and the Beast"

When we were children, most of our parents told us dozens or even hundreds of fairy tales. But did you know that among these stories, we could see a part of feminism? That's what we're going to talk about today, and more particularly about the adaptation of "Beauty and the Beast" in the cinema.

When the princess saves the prince

Beauty and the Beast is basically a tale about a young woman called "the beauty" who wanted to save her father who was sentenced to death for picking a rose in the domain of a terrible monster called "the beast". The beautiful one then sacrificed herself to this one by exchanging her life against that of her father. But the beast decided to spare the beauty and allowed her to live in his castle. It is thus by living side by side that the beautiful one realized that behind the features of the wild animal, hid in fact a man suffering and victim of a spell. This tale has been the subject of many adaptations, whether in film, theater or books, the most famous being the adaptation made for children by the Disney studios. This tale is of a very new kind, it is one of the first where the princess saves the prince, and not the other way around. Indeed, in a first time it is she who saves the beast from the terrible Gaston, the antagonist of the story. Then in a second time, it is she who delivers him from the curse of which he was victim.

Trailer of the film in French

Books and knowledge, an independence

This part of the story is more developed in the very recent film adaptation where the actress and feminist Emma Watson plays the main character "Belle". According to the actress, "Belle's thirst for knowledge, which she quenches through books, plays a major role in her independence." The young woman accumulating knowledge will go so far as to create a washing machine, thus freeing women from household chores. This invention allows women to learn to read and go to school. However, if the women welcome the invention with open arms, it is far from pleasing the villagers who will destroy it thereafter. This is explained by the fact that the inhabitants do not think that a woman should be able to read or write, they are suspicious of intelligence. A woman with knowledge at that time was very badly perceived by men. By breaking this washing machine, they hope to break the spirit of Belle becoming more and more independent and thus push her to become a "normal" woman again.

Other examples

Beauty and the Beast is not the only work where the female character shines on the male character, so we will see some of these works. To start, one of Disney's strongest heroines, Pocahontas. A young woman fighting to keep the peace between her Indian people and the colonists who invade America. Indeed, it is Pocahontas who saves John Smith on many occasions and not the other way around. The work contains many lessons about love, respect and equality. To continue, Merida from the Disney movie "Rebel" is also a great example of a strong female character. A woman who has no say in front of her parents, her problem being an aranged marriage. The strongest one during a festival will receive Merida's hand, however she will go as far as to participate herself in this festival of which she is the prize to get out of this situation. How to talk about strong women in Disney movies without mentioning Mulan? Heroine who will go as far as to impersonate a man to save the whole of China. She will prove to us throughout the adventure that a woman can do everything exactly like a man and even better! Mulan doesn't care about gender stereotypes, she commands respect.
