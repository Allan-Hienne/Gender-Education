In the 15th century

Jean de Gerson, grand chancellor of the University of Paris, declares: "All teaching for women must be considered suspect".

XVIIIth century

To the theories of Diderot, who asserted that the subordination of women to men was a tyranny, was opposed the antifeminism of Rousseau, for whom this sex could not claim equality, especially in education.

1808

Girls and women are not allowed on school grounds.

1850

The Falloux law requires communes with more than 800 inhabitants to open a school for girls.

1882

The Ferry law recognizes the equality of the sexes in education.

1924

Unification of programs for girls and boys.

1938

Women can enroll in university without their husband's permission (removal of civil incapacity).

1989

The law of orientation on education recalls the mission of coeducation and equality of education. Schools "contribute to promoting equality between men and women".
